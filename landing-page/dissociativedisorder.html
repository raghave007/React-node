Complex PTSD and Dissociative Disorder: Treating More Than Trauma Symptoms

According to National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI):

  • over 6 million people in the U.S. experience dissociative disorders;
  • about 8 million people are experiencing PTSD; and,
  • 35% of those - almost 3 million - are experiencing the most severe symptoms that may be diagnosed as complex PTSD.

These symptoms are primarily a response to trauma. As a therapist, you see clients dealing with these issues every day. Your challenge lies in identifying and treating the root cause - processing the trauma - not just the trauma symptoms. This is where the Instinctual Trauma Response (ITR) method can help.

What makes the ITR method different?

The ITR evidence-supported treatment method takes a different approach to treating trauma. Instead of asking “What’s wrong with the client?” the question becomes “What’s happened to the client?”.

A crucial element, the ITR method enables the client to process through the event without having to relive the trauma. The ITR method provides structure, words and historical context to traumatic events. Additionally, the approach benefits include:

  • Treatment time to success - hours vs years: Unlike many psychodynamic approaches, processing a single traumatic event can happen quickly, with impact in as little as one session.
  • Integrates seamlessly with other therapeutic models: Regardless of your psychodynamic or psychoanalytic approach, the ITR method adapts to become part of your process. 
  • Can be used with both children and adults: This method can be applied to clients as young as 3 - or 93. 

Dissociative disorder, complex PTSD and dissociative regression

Dissociative regression occurs as a complication of trauma and is frequently seen in complex post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and dissociative disorders. In this form of regression a person loses the normal capacity for self-regulation and executive functioning because of the failure of one or more fundamental psychological functions. The regression needs to be reversed before the underlying trauma can be addressed. The ITR method provides a regimen that can be done in a few weeks without hospitalization and includes the family in the process of helping their loved one.
